# Tourist Japanese Resources

Curated, high-trust only. Knowledge in lessons is drawn from here, not from guesswork.

## Knowledge

- [Tofugu — Learn Hiragana: The Ultimate Guide](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese/learn-hiragana/)
  Mnemonic-driven guide that gets you reading hiragana fast. Use for: the hiragana track, pronunciation basics.
- [Tofugu — Learn Katakana: The Ultimate Guide](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese/learn-katakana/)
  Same method for katakana (the pointy script used for loanwords — highest sign-reading ROI for tourists). Use for: the katakana track.
- [Tofugu — Kana Quiz (web app)](https://kana-quiz.tofugu.com/)
  Free self-testing tool for both kana. Use for: daily 3-minute spaced retrieval practice.
- [Tae Kim — Guide to Japanese Grammar](https://guidetojapanese.org/learn/grammar/)
  The clearest free grammar reference; explains particles (は wa, を o, が ga) by *function*, not translation. Use for: understanding *why* a pattern works. Note: teaches casual→polite; we invert (polite first) for tourist use.
- [Tae Kim — The major particles](https://guidetojapanese.org/particles.html)
  Deep dive on は/を/が. Use for: the "particles = articles" analogy.
- [All About Japan — 33 Survival Kanji for Japan First-Timers](https://allabout-japan.com/en/article/4836/)
  The exact kanji you'll see on doors, platforms, shops. Use for: the survival-kanji reference.
- [Onigiri Anki — 25 Essential Japanese Kanji Signs (2026)](https://www.onigiri-anki.com/blog/japanese-kanji-signs-guide)
  Sign-reading logic (e.g. 北口/南口/東口/西口 = compass + 口 mouth = exits). Use for: recognising station exits and prohibition signs by shape.
- [The Invisible Tourist — 37 Useful Japanese Phrases (+ free cheat sheet PDF)](https://www.theinvisibletourist.com/japanese-for-tourists/)
  Traveller-tested phrase list with a printable PDF. Use for: cross-checking which phrases are actually high-frequency.
- [JR Rail Pass — 20 Basic Japanese Phrases for Traveling](https://www.jrailpass.com/blog/basic-japanese-phrases-for-traveling)
  Transport-focused phrases. Use for: the transportation vocab domain.

### History / etymology (for the "backstory" asides)

- [Japanese writing system — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_writing_system)
  Overview of kanji/hiragana/katakana origins and the division of labour. Use for: "why three scripts" asides.
- [Hiragana — Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/hiragana) · [Katakana — Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/katakana)
  High-trust encyclopaedia entries on how each kana developed from man'yōgana. Use for: script-history facts.
- [Loanwords in Japanese — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loanwords_in_Japanese)
  Portuguese/Dutch/English borrowing waves, gairaigo vs kango, ateji. Use for: "why borrowed vs invented" asides.
- [Dakuten and handakuten — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dakuten_and_handakuten)
  The voicing diacritic (゛/゜); historically optional. Use for: "why ガ is カ + two dots" asides.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/LearnJapanese](https://www.reddit.com/r/LearnJapanese/)
  Large, heavily-moderated subreddit with a strong beginner wiki and a Daily Thread for quick questions. Use for: "is my sentence right?", pronunciation checks, resource sanity-checks.
- [Tatoeba](https://tatoeba.org/en/)
  Corpus of example sentences with audio, many by native speakers. Use for: hearing a real word/pattern in context before you use it.
- [HelloTalk / Tandem (apps)](https://www.hellotalk.com/)
  Language-exchange apps to trade voice messages with native speakers. Use for: real feedback on your spoken transactions before the trip.
