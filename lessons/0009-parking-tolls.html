駐車場 — parking, tolls & signs

Driving in Japan is mostly a reading test. Learn a handful of sign shapes and the coin-parking pay flow, and you can find a spot, pay for it, and get through a highway toll gate without stress.

The two signs you must read: 満車 vs 空車

Every parking lot shows one of these at the entrance. This is the single most important read.

SignRomajiMeansSay
満車manshaFULL — keep driving, no spaces
空車kūshaVACANT — spaces available, go in
You already know half of this. 空 (kū / aki) is the same "empty / vacant" you learned for a free toilet stall in Lesson 6. Here it's 空 + 車 (car) = "empty of cars." And 満 (full) is the 満 from 満タン (full tank) last lesson.

The coin-parking pay flow

Coin parking (the small unattended lots) works the same everywhere: you pay after, on the way out.1

  1. Park in a numbered space. A flap or camera records your car.
  2. Before leaving, go to the 精算機 (payment machine).
  3. Enter your space number, and it shows the 料金 (fee). Pay with coins, notes, or often a card.
  4. You then have a few minutes to drive out.

If you can't find the machine, use Pattern B from Lesson 4:

精算機はどこですか？
Seisanki wa doko desu ka?  ·  "Where is the payment machine?"

And to ask a person the fee, the new word is いくら (ikura, "how much"): いくらですか？ = "How much is it?"

Highway tolls: ETC vs 一般

At a toll gate (料金所 ryōkinjo ) you choose a lane by colour and sign:2

Lane signColourUse it if…
ETCpurpleyou have the ETC card (from Lesson 8) — just drive through slowly, it pays automatically
一般 (ippan)greenno ETC — stop, take a ticket on the way in, pay cash at the exit gate
The simple plan: rent the ETC card, always pick the purple ETC lane, and pay all your tolls at once when you return the car. If you skipped the card, follow the green 一般 lane and keep the ticket it gives you.
📜 Backstory · 満車 / 空車 are "full of cars / empty of cars"

Both end in 車 (sha, "vehicle") — the same 車 in 駐車場 (parking lot) and 電車 (train). 満 (full) + 車 = full of cars; 空 (empty) + 車 = empty of cars. Read the first character and you know if you can park. This 車 is one of the most useful kanji shapes to recognise on the road. Survival kanji →
