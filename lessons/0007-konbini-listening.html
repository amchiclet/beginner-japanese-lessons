コンビニ — catching the clerk's keyword

The convenience store is your most frequent interaction in Japan, and the clerk fires the same three or four quick questions every time. You don't need to understand the whole polite sentence. Catch one keyword, answer with one of two words, and you're through.

Warm-up: recall from before

From memory first, then tap to check.

The two answers that handle almost everything

Most clerk questions are yes/no. Learn this pair and you can answer nearly all of them:

WordRomajiMeansSay
お願いしますonegaishimasuYes, please (I'll take it)
大丈夫ですdaijōbu desuNo, I'm fine (polite "no thanks")
大丈夫です is the tourist's best friend. It's a soft, polite "no thank you" for almost any offer: a bag, heating, a receipt, chopsticks. Catch the keyword, then just say お願いします (yes) or 大丈夫です (no).

The clerk's questions — catch the keyword

When you walk in you'll hear いらっしゃいませ (welcome). Ignore it, no reply needed. Then at the register, listen for the highlighted word:

They say (🔊)Listen forAskingYou answer
袋はいりますか？
fukuro wa irimasu ka?		 袋 (fukuro)Do you need a bag? お願いします / 大丈夫です
温めますか？
atatamemasu ka?		 温め (atatame)Heat it up? (bento, etc.) お願いします / 大丈夫です
お箸はおつけしますか？
o-hashi wa otsuke shimasu ka?		 お箸 (o-hashi)Add chopsticks? お願いします / 大丈夫です
ポイントカードはお持ちですか？
pointo kādo wa o-mochi desu ka?		 ポイントカードGot a point card? 大丈夫です (tourists: no)
お支払いは現金ですか、カードですか？
o-shiharai wa genkin desu ka, kādo desu ka?		 現金 / カードCash or card? 現金で / カードで
レシートはご利用ですか？
reshīto wa go-riyō desu ka?		 レシートDo you want the receipt? お願いします / 大丈夫です
For cash or card, answer with the word + で (de, "with"): 現金で (with cash) or カードで (with card).
📜 Backstory · why they always ask about the 袋

They didn't always ask. In July 2020 Japan brought in a nationwide charge for plastic shopping bags (レジ袋有料化, reji-bukuro yūryōka), a few yen each, to cut waste. Overnight, "袋はいりますか？" became a question every clerk asks every customer. So this keyword is recent, and now unavoidable. More listening keywords →
