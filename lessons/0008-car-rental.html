レンタカー — renting a car

Rental staff usually won't speak much English, and the counter has a predictable script: your licence, insurance, the fuel policy, and a couple of add-ons. Catch the keyword, answer short, and produce three phrases of your own. Then you're on the road.

Warm-up: recall from before

From memory first, then tap to check.

Before you go (not language, but essential): to drive in Japan you need an International Driving Permit (国際免許証) from your home country, plus your home licence and passport. No IDP, no car.1

What the clerk will ask — catch the keyword

Same listening skill as the konbini. Their sentences are polite and long; you just need the keyword.

They say (🔊)Listen forAskingYou answer
免許証をお願いします。
menkyoshō o onegaishimasu		 免許証 (menkyoshō)your licence, please (hand over IDP + licence) はい
保険はどうしますか？
hoken wa dō shimasu ka?		 保険 (hoken)what about insurance? お願いします (yes, add it — recommended)
満タンで返してください。
mantan de kaeshite kudasai		 満タン (mantan)return it with a full tank はい (fill up before returning!)
何時に返しますか？
nan-ji ni kaeshimasu ka?		 何時 (nan-ji)what time will you return it? (say a time — numbers lesson coming)
満タン返し (mantan-gaeshi) is the standard fuel rule: bring the car back with a full tank, or they charge you (usually more than the pump). Fill up at a gas station right before you return it.2

Three phrases to produce

You start by claiming your booking (from Lesson 6), then two useful requests:

予約しています。
Yoyaku shite imasu.  ·  "I have a reservation." (add your name: ___ です)
ナビを英語にできますか？
Navi o eigo ni dekimasu ka?  ·  "Can you set the GPS to English?"
ETCカードをお願いします。
ETC kādo o onegaishimasu.  ·  "An ETC card, please." (for automatic highway tolls — Lesson 9)
Get the ETC card. It's a cheap rental (~¥330) that lets you glide through highway tolls in the ETC lane and settle everything when you return the car. Much easier than paying cash at every toll gate.3 More on that next lesson.
📜 Backstory · 免許証 = "permission paper"

免許証 breaks into 免 (exempt/permit) + 許 (allow) + 証 (certificate) — a "permission certificate." The same 証 (proof/certificate) shows up on other IDs like 学生証 (student ID) and 保険証 (insurance card). Spot 証 on the end of a word and it's usually some kind of official card. More kanji logic →
