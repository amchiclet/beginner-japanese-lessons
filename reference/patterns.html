The 3 core sentence patterns

Almost every tourist interaction is one of these three frames with a different noun dropped into the slot. Learn the frames once; swap the nouns forever.

The big idea (your "articles"). Japanese has no a/the. Instead, small words called particles come after a word to mark its job in the sentence. Get the particle right and the sentence works. は (wa) marks the topic ("as for…"), を (o) marks the object ("the thing acted on"). Note: は is written with the ha character but pronounced wa as a particle.

Pattern A — request / order

___ を ください
___ o kudasai  ·  "___, please" / "I'll have ___"
literally: [thing] + (object marker) + please-give-me

The workhorse of shopping and ordering. Point at anything and say これをください (kore o kudasai) = "this, please."

Pattern B — locate

___ は どこ です か？
___ wa doko desu ka?  ·  "where is ___?"
literally: [thing] + (topic marker) + where + is + (question marker)

Finding the toilet, the station, the exit. トイレはどこですか？ (toire wa doko desu ka?) = "where is the toilet?"

Pattern C — availability

___ は あります か？
___ wa arimasu ka?  ·  "do you have ___? / is there ___?"
literally: [thing] + (topic marker) + exists + (question marker)

Asking a shop or hotel if something exists. Wi-Fiはありますか？ (wai-fai wa arimasu ka?) = "do you have Wi-Fi?"

The glue words

KanaRomajiJob
ですdesu"is / it is" — polite copula, ends statements
かkaturns a statement into a question (like a spoken "?")
くださいkudasai"please give me" — ends a request
すみませんsumimasen"excuse me" — open any interaction with this
はwaparticle: marks the topic ("as for ___")
をoparticle: marks the object ("___ is what I want")
Politeness shortcut. Start with すみません (sumimasen), end with the right glue word (kudasai to request, desu ka to ask), and you sound polite without any verb conjugation.
