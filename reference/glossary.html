Tourist Japanese · Reference

Glossary

The running word + term list for the course. Every lesson draws from and adds to this. Romaji is a pronunciation crutch for speaking; the kana column is what you'll actually read on signs.

Pronunciation is steady: a="ah", i="ee", u="oo", e="eh", o="oh". Every letter is said. ō / ū mean the vowel is held longer.

Grammar terms

TermMeans
particlea small word placed after a noun to mark its job (topic, object…). Japanese's replacement for word order + articles.
topic (は wa)"as for ___" — what the sentence is about.
object (を o)the thing an action is done to (the thing you want).
copula (です desu)the polite "is / it is" that ends a statement.
kanathe two phonetic scripts: hiragana (native words, grammar) and katakana (loanwords).
kanjiChinese-origin characters. We only learn a handful as sign shapes.

Lesson 3 · katakana K-row + dakuten (reading track)

KatakanaRomajiNote
カ キ ク ケ コka ki ku ke koK-row; Japanese k is unaspirated (Thai ก). コ starts コーヒー.
ガ ギ グ ゲ ゴga gi gu ge goK-row + dakuten ゛ (voiced). ゲ starts ゲーム.
゛dakuten"two dots" mark that voices a consonant: k→g, and later s→z, t→d, h→b.

Whole words now readable: ケーキ (cake), ココア (cocoa), キウイ (kiwi), オーケー (OK), ギア (gear).
